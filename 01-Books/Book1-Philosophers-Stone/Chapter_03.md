# Chapter 3 — The Letters From No One

The escape of the Brazilian boa constrictor <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="663546de-5fc2-44ce-bcba-fc94d3042b46">earned</mark> Harry his longest-ever punishment. By the time he was allowed out of his cupboard again, the summer holidays had started and Dudley had already broken his new video camera, crashed his remote control airplane, and, first time out on his racing bike, knocked down old Mrs. Figg as she crossed Privet Drive on her <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="5d5a7640-fb18-46aa-8287-65fb182204d7">crutches</mark>.

Harry was glad school was over, but there was no escaping Dudley’s gang, who visited the house every single day. Piers, Dennis, Malcolm, and Gordon were all big and stupid, but as Dudley was the biggest and stupidest of the lot, he was the leader. The rest of them were all quite happy to join in Dudley’s favorite sport: Harry Hunting.

This was why Harry spent as much time as possible out of the house, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ec354f52-ed36-4751-bae1-16ef3f18e044">wandering</mark> around and thinking about the end of the holidays, where he could see <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-blue" data-id="94e69c44-6c81-4c10-b446-12d2c97fb8bd">a tiny ray of hope</mark>. When September came he would be going off to secondary school and, for the first time in his life, he wouldn’t be with Dudley. Dudley had been accepted at Uncle Vernon’s old private school, Smeltings. Piers Polkiss was going there too. Harry, on the other hand, was going to Stonewall High, the local public school. Dudley thought this was very funny.

“They <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="5e89d252-5822-4452-9c82-4c4b9cbc0512">stuff</mark> people’s heads down the toilet the first day at Stonewall,” he told Harry. “Want to come upstairs and practice?”

<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-pink" data-id="04838717-0dd1-40cd-b629-b235313b6916">“No, thanks,” said Harry. “The poor toilet’s never had anything as horrible as your head down it — it might be sick.” Then he ran, before Dudley could work out what he’d said.</mark>

One day in July, Aunt Petunia took Dudley to London to buy his Smeltings uniform, leaving Harry at Mrs. Figg’s. Mrs. Figg wasn’t as bad as usual. It turned out she’d broken her leg tripping over one of her cats, and she didn’t seem quite as fond of them as before. She let Harry watch television and gave him a bit of chocolate cake that tasted as though she’d had it for several years.

That evening, Dudley <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="f63d2a8d-3aab-4b56-b4af-4cbd4e66bcdf">paraded</mark> around the living room for the family in his brand-new uniform. Smeltings boys wore <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="59222cb5-91fc-4324-8590-b093eb66d1e8">maroon</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="469df408-18bd-4c27-af6a-34dc4ccfdc49">tailcoats</mark>, orange <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d00f637e-c57c-46ef-a646-39197fc2c1e6">knickerbockers</mark>, and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="54420fa1-3661-412c-b547-7121045f4c4b">flat</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="0775ebb9-90c5-4545-8da1-b63c9afcfe14">straw</mark> hats called <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="07d1f1d6-0d22-4add-b694-19a53a6658c8">boaters</mark>. They also carried knobbly sticks, used for hitting each other while the teachers weren’t looking. This was supposed to be good training for later life.

As he looked at Dudley in his new knickerbockers, Uncle Vernon said <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1d609aad-490e-4998-965e-87b36a946ec5">gruffly</mark> that it was the proudest moment of his life. Aunt Petunia <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c1eae3ce-9228-4933-8311-39a73a633b0d">burst</mark> into tears and said she couldn’t believe it was her Ickle Dudleykins, he looked so handsome and grown-up. <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-pink" data-id="01eebadc-0b43-460f-98f6-bb01aad79ac8">Harry didn’t trust himself to speak. He thought two of his ribs might already have cracked from trying not to laugh.</mark>

There was a horrible smell in the kitchen the next morning when Harry went in for breakfast. It seemed to be coming from a large <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2f361542-1255-4259-a12c-eb69bf1c341e">metal tub</mark> in the sink. He went to have a look. The tub was full of what looked like dirty rags swimming in gray water.

“What’s this?” he asked Aunt Petunia. Her lips <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="608898ce-d780-446b-b7eb-65eb66fcbeaf">tightened</mark> as they always did if he dared to ask a question.

“Your new school uniform,” she said.

Harry looked in the bowl again.

“Oh,” he said, “I didn’t realize it had to be so wet.”

“Don’t be stupid,” snapped Aunt Petunia. “I’m <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="3eceffa1-145e-423a-9a29-73a83e6e86c8">dyeing</mark> some of Dudley’s old things gray for you. It’ll look just like everyone else’s when I’ve finished.”

Harry seriously doubted this, but thought it best not to argue. He sat down at the table and tried not to think about how he was going to look on his first day at Stonewall High — like he was wearing <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="56e00f15-69fb-410e-9283-d58048c06489">bits of</mark> old elephant skin, probably.

Dudley and Uncle Vernon came in, both with <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1cf88308-d27c-40e6-ba11-ce97ebd4b44f">wrinkled</mark> noses because of the smell from Harry’s new uniform. Uncle Vernon opened his newspaper as usual and Dudley banged his Smelting stick, which he carried everywhere, on the table.

They heard the click of the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="38ea0ae5-9fec-41e0-bb43-b7a8d6a107f1">mail slot</mark> and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="92ea4116-dadd-45ef-a596-d4e14cecc4f6">flop</mark> of letters on the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="5954d823-cc91-41c1-811d-1095f3d01b39">doormat</mark>.

“Get the mail, Dudley,” said Uncle Vernon from behind his paper.

“Make Harry get it.”

“Get the mail, Harry.”

“Make Dudley get it.”

“<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="eade8473-1915-4448-ab87-9d00fbdb848c">Poke</mark> him with your Smelting stick, Dudley.”

Harry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6ed42923-4711-440c-9e1b-e0868ec0192f">dodged</mark> the Smelting stick and went to get the mail. Three things lay on the doormat: a postcard from Uncle Vernon’s sister Marge, who was vacationing on the Isle of Wight, a brown <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="b0a0ccfe-0a5d-4b10-9d0a-a9b85edfb5c9">envelope</mark> that looked like a bill, and — a letter for Harry.

Harry picked it up and stared at it, his heart <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="0ca55775-acd2-4cd7-9d69-d5299cc93d86">twanging</mark> like a giant <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="0e129b50-3fd4-4e0c-9a78-f4c0719fc9ed">elastic band</mark>. No one, ever, in his whole life, had written to him. Who would? He had no friends, no other relatives — he didn’t belong to the library, so he’d never even got rude notes asking for books back. Yet here it was, a letter, addressed so <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6f868fc2-3ea8-4c87-a010-1b572defc00a">plainly</mark> there could be no mistake:

Mr. H. Potter

The Cupboard under the Stairs

4 Privet Drive

Little Whinging

Surrey

The <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="56f09a71-d904-49e7-8f45-e59854d79b60">envelope</mark> was thick and heavy, made of <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="18d48df0-e7ae-456e-b7a8-a53f82741f9f">yellowish</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a0f02876-c5b8-46a1-a9a1-3adca88360e2">parchment</mark>, and the address was written in emerald-green ink. There was no stamp.

Turning the envelope over, his hand trembling, Harry saw <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="bb8e5b8a-73c0-4033-9494-2f793b883177">a purple wax seal bearing a coat of arms</mark>; a lion, an eagle, a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ef7a234e-f61f-4cdc-9aba-e17ffb34777d">badger</mark>, and a snake surrounding a large letter H.

“Hurry up, boy!” shouted Uncle Vernon from the kitchen. “What are you doing, checking for letter bombs?” He <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="bed59321-0507-466d-af71-7f0edf85743d">chuckled</mark> at his own joke.

Harry went back to the kitchen, still staring at his letter. He handed Uncle Vernon the bill and the postcard, sat down, and slowly began to open the yellow envelope.

Uncle Vernon <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c81e5677-d02d-47bb-81fd-8ac4ac06156f">ripped</mark> open the bill, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="f1eb5b91-11df-429e-881c-c78e1d41340f">snorted</mark> in disgust, and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="b3e89ff2-59e7-4c47-b0d9-d2508b583f8f">flipped</mark> over the postcard.

“Marge’s ill,” he informed Aunt Petunia. “Ate a funny <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2198112a-9ec4-41ce-8948-03922de71569">whelk</mark> …”

“Dad!” said Dudley suddenly. “Dad, Harry’s got something!”

Harry was on the point of <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="4f656409-f33d-4493-a608-70c68544b80a">unfolding</mark> his letter, which was written on the same heavy <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c3f0724d-391a-460a-9450-42313efe8f8a">parchment</mark> as the envelope, when it was jerked sharply out of his hand by Uncle Vernon.

“That’s mine!” said Harry, trying to <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="95798148-c96b-4b98-a6b6-424a1b2d5c6e">snatch</mark> it back.

“Who’d be writing to you?” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="bb2a0490-d631-4ef1-859e-247d3d8430da">sneered</mark> Uncle Vernon, shaking the letter open with one hand and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="758eee90-2b7d-4648-90ce-5fe01464b78e">glancing</mark> at it. <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-blue" data-id="e7394838-3429-40cf-bb5d-2ddd92a26c84">His face went from red to green faster than a set of traffic lights.</mark> And it didn’t stop there. Within seconds it was the grayish white of old <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="70cf9ec9-33d4-4f41-b5c1-143ad525ce0a">porridge</mark>.

“P-P-Petunia!” he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6696c8b5-7013-4d28-910f-dd875b3e9f05">gasped</mark>.

Dudley tried to <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d80a2147-636b-4dbc-aea4-d21d788b5d52">grab</mark> the letter to read it, but Uncle Vernon held it high out of his reach. Aunt Petunia took it curiously and read the first line. For a moment it looked as though she might <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="e1f248dc-5193-4afd-b123-cbb4199c5ef7">faint</mark>. She <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="dabf83f9-22c2-4c88-a16f-85ab10b6b67a">clutched</mark> her throat and made a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="04ebe8ea-a214-4938-b6c8-c5ce198789ce">choking</mark> noise.

“Vernon! Oh my goodness — Vernon!”

They stared at each other, seeming to have forgotten that Harry and Dudley were still in the room. Dudley wasn’t used to being ignored. He gave his father <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="a7fbb71f-0654-4838-81dc-1197c141e23a">a sharp tap</mark> on the head with his Smelting stick.

“I want to read that letter,” he said loudly.

“I want to read it,” said Harry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="e922b4f0-8674-43e9-a067-25356ebd1d07">furiously</mark>, “as it’s mine.”

“Get out, both of you,” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="eafa9836-9687-41e1-bb3b-737fa37c77f7">croaked</mark> Uncle Vernon, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="47421634-d1c1-4f7f-b779-57b0d6dd56b2">stuffing</mark> the letter back inside its envelope.

Harry didn’t move.

“I WANT MY LETTER!” he shouted.

“Let me see it!” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c73d7176-5cd0-4e91-9aa0-863089a4cd78">demanded</mark> Dudley.

“OUT!” roared Uncle Vernon, and he took both Harry and Dudley <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="ca1699b5-4169-4cce-b9a9-28802585252f">by the scruffs of their necks</mark> and threw them into the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="3bc384f7-5a98-4539-9514-b82761c13d29">hall</mark>, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="cbcaafdb-e72f-4b6f-9984-b9fcd4e2a380">slamming</mark> the kitchen door behind them. Harry and Dudley <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a066a911-0dd7-4471-acce-ed00fd644950">promptly</mark> had a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ba6dd88f-24c7-4d5a-b933-3aab42c94cd3">furious</mark> but silent fight over who would listen at the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="8cb880bb-b236-4fba-9e37-318bc0ce548d">keyhole</mark>; Dudley won, so Harry, his glasses <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="dcd0a9dd-3ead-405f-a9d8-b2bb94d9b394">dangling</mark> from one ear, lay flat on his stomach to listen at the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="e25ef618-6723-4b2b-a6fe-8f2500ddceee">crack</mark> between door and floor.

“Vernon,” Aunt Petunia was saying in a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="370378ac-94de-463e-ab73-ade7e53b2b21">quivering</mark> voice, “look at the address — how could they possibly know where he sleeps? You don’t think they’re watching the house?”

“Watching — spying — might be following us,” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6a6d8007-7aef-49a4-8f33-9ce7697a872e">muttered</mark> Uncle Vernon <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="689ab0e4-87bc-46ef-a4de-30db9d2feb2e">wildly</mark>.

“But what should we do, Vernon? Should we write back? Tell them we don’t want —”

Harry could see Uncle Vernon’s <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a6682494-0cc7-42eb-ad55-09cc2bb58e6b">shiny</mark> black shoes <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d5854694-e7e0-4e38-8367-2babe8983ebc">pacing</mark> up and down the kitchen.

“No,” he said finally. “No, we’ll ignore it. If they don’t get an answer. … Yes, that’s best … we won’t do anything. …”

“But —”

“I’m not having one in the house, Petunia! Didn’t we swear when we took him in we’d <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d27be1db-3ffc-421b-9abf-1a1654d08e3b">stamp out</mark> that dangerous nonsense?”

That evening when he got back from work, Uncle Vernon did something he’d never done before; he visited Harry in his cupboard.

“Where’s my letter?” said Harry, the moment Uncle Vernon had <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="48aa838f-407b-439e-a725-202fe4a32e91">squeezed</mark> through the door. “Who’s writing to me?”

“No one. It was addressed to you by mistake,” said Uncle Vernon shortly. “I have burned it.”

“It was not a mistake,” said Harry angrily, “it had my cupboard on it.”

“SILENCE!” yelled Uncle Vernon, and a couple of spiders fell from the ceiling. He took a few deep breaths and then forced his face into a smile, which looked quite painful.

“Er — yes, Harry — about this cupboard. Your aunt and I have been thinking … you’re really getting a bit big for it … we think it might be nice if you moved into Dudley’s second bedroom.”

“Why?” said Harry.

“Don’t ask questions!” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="59416b16-ddf4-4745-ae51-302ca793ec03">snapped</mark> his uncle. “Take this stuff upstairs, now.”

The Dursleys’ house had four bedrooms: one for Uncle Vernon and Aunt Petunia, one for visitors (usually Uncle Vernon’s sister, Marge), one where Dudley slept, and one where Dudley kept all the toys and things that wouldn’t fit into his first bedroom. It only took Harry one trip upstairs to move everything he owned from the cupboard to this room. He sat down on the bed and stared around him. Nearly everything in here was broken. The month-old video camera was lying on top of a small, working tank Dudley had once driven over the next door neighbor’s dog; in the corner was Dudley’s first-ever television set, which he’d put his foot through when his favorite program had been canceled; there was a large birdcage, which had once held a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7c8a58a8-5fbb-455a-b56f-93c56c28e87b">parrot</mark> that Dudley had <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="68fd75d0-3f24-4653-8159-19d0e5e7c08a">swapped</mark> at school for a real air <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="3a7cfe3e-5f56-4986-b257-7e17ee088a2c">rifle</mark>, which was up on a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="f4c30b88-5064-402a-8a17-9ef3ab7386f5">shelf</mark> with the end all <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ab9be685-3c23-4720-9cb3-109c9c3e2688">bent</mark> because Dudley had sat on it. Other shelves were full of books. They were the only things in the room that looked as though they’d never been touched.

From downstairs came the sound of Dudley <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="8871f190-7cc6-44a5-a19a-cf58d4d75700">bawling</mark> at his mother, “I don’t want him in there … I need that room … make him get out. …”

Harry sighed and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="0712972e-e993-4f81-b4c5-8060eb35e107">stretched</mark> out on the bed. Yesterday he’d have given anything to be up here. Today he’d rather be back in his cupboard with that letter than up here without it.

Next morning at breakfast, everyone was rather quiet. Dudley was in shock. He’d screamed, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="14003405-a172-49dd-b5eb-273056dc8e98">whacked</mark> his father with his Smelting stick, been sick on purpose, kicked his mother, and thrown his <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="fb5c30cd-2910-46b6-ad51-346c79788aa3">tortoise</mark> through the greenhouse roof, and he still didn’t have his room back. Harry was thinking about this time yesterday and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="56350d70-a078-4d15-a07b-df1548ba4e76">bitterly</mark> wishing he’d opened the letter in the hall. Uncle Vernon and Aunt Petunia kept looking at each other darkly.

When the post arrived, Uncle Vernon, who seemed to be trying to be nice to Harry, made Dudley go and get it. They heard him banging things with his Smelting stick all the way down the hall. Then he shouted, “There’s another one! ‘Mr. H. Potter, The Smallest Bedroom, 4 Privet Drive —’ ”

With a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d28e7871-2392-47c0-aea0-744eae3db8ef">strangled</mark> cry, Uncle Vernon <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="07a9d2be-1428-44be-a9ea-5892b690ed1f">leapt</mark> from his seat and ran down the hall, Harry right behind him. Uncle Vernon had to <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="63cbe514-1b0c-4f77-8a54-17a40f7eb1e1">wrestle</mark> Dudley to the ground to get the letter from him, which was made difficult by the fact that Harry had <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="b6671664-80a9-4487-89a9-55ecdb6f4d51">grabbed</mark> Uncle Vernon around the neck from behind. After a minute of confused fighting, in which everyone got hit a lot by the Smelting stick, Uncle Vernon <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="41b16efc-b78b-4ce5-8589-e4392fb9d060">straightened</mark> up, gasping for breath, with Harry’s letter <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2a98fc06-373e-450d-b9a8-737da15abe33">clutched</mark> in his hand.

“Go to your cupboard — I mean, your bedroom,” he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="9ae9bb66-71c4-4dd0-bb48-a4be24c262b9">wheezed</mark> at Harry. “Dudley — go — just go.”

Harry walked round and round his new room. Someone knew he had moved out of his cupboard and they seemed to know he hadn’t received his first letter. Surely that meant they’d try again? And this time he’d make sure they didn’t fail. He had a plan.

The repaired <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d6d9b09d-be3f-486d-81bf-1e1ec1dd38a2">alarm</mark> clock rang at six o’clock the next morning. Harry turned it off quickly and dressed silently He mustn’t wake the Dursleys. He <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="4e8959cc-e301-4b4b-b131-aa961d3581bb">stole</mark> downstairs without turning on any of the lights.

He was going to wait for the postman on the corner of Privet Drive and get the letters for number four first. His heart hammered as he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="118c8b76-326c-4e19-9584-7f20436dbdfa">crept</mark> across the dark hall toward the front door —

“AAAAARRRGH!”

Harry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="483e2cc1-bc9a-4ff5-9525-7de8e590a533">leapt</mark> into the air; he’d <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="5697ca1a-a833-4ee6-94a5-f0901cb7f170">trodden</mark> on something big and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="88234a74-877b-4834-a27f-482fb6a6e5a3">squashy</mark> on the doormat — something alive!

Lights clicked on upstairs and to his horror Harry realized that the big, squashy something had been his uncle’s face. Uncle Vernon had been lying at the foot of the front door in a sleeping bag, clearly making sure that Harry didn’t do exactly what he’d been trying to do. He shouted at Harry for about half an hour and then told him to go and make a cup of tea. Harry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="e902ed58-8a91-4b80-af8b-431566484959">shuffled</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2c7beb5b-1815-409a-8510-3c0f10a001c9">miserably</mark> off into the kitchen and by the time he got back, the post had arrived, right into Uncle Vernon’s <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="43a173cf-7e07-4267-81d9-ebb819694d4f">lap</mark>. Harry could see three letters addressed in green ink.

“I want —” he began, but Uncle Vernon was tearing the letters into pieces before his eyes.

Uncle Vernon didn’t go to work that day. He stayed at home and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="393e94c2-cfcb-4eee-9040-5a90169c91f6">nailed</mark> up the letter-box.

- **a tiny ray of hope**
  - 含义：一线希望
  - 用法：a ray of hope，比喻极小但珍贵的希望
  - 模仿：The scholarship offered a tiny ray of hope for her future.

  - 含义：他的脸从红变绿比红绿灯变得还快
  - 用法：用红绿灯比喻脸色急剧变化，幽默夸张
  - 模仿：Her face went from red to white faster than a set of traffic lights when she saw the bill.

### 句型 / 语法（Green）

- **bits of**（短语）
  - 含义：一块块；一片片
  - 结构 / 用法：bits of + 不可数名词，表示碎块、碎片
  - 例：The shirt looked like bits of old elephant skin.
- **a purple wax seal bearing a coat of arms**（短语）
  - 含义：带有纹章的紫色蜡封
  - 结构 / 用法：bearing 为现在分词作后置定语，修饰 seal；coat of arms 指纹章
  - 例：The document bore a wax seal bearing a coat of arms.
- **a sharp tap**（短语）
  - 含义：猛地一敲
  - 结构 / 用法：adj. + n.，sharp 表示突然、用力
  - 例：He gave his father a sharp tap on the head with his Smelting stick.
- **by the scruffs of their necks**（习语）
  - 含义：抓住后颈
  - 结构 / 用法：by the scruff of the neck，指抓动物或人后颈皮
  - 例：The teacher grabbed the boys by the scruffs of their necks and threw them out.
- **slipping and sliding**（短语动词）
  - 含义：跌跌撞撞；一路打滑
  - 结构 / 用法：slip and slide 并列，形容在湿滑表面行走
  - 例：Uncle Vernon, slipping and sliding, led the way to the broken-down house.
- **wrenched their way**（短语动词）
  - 含义：费力地挤过
  - 结构 / 用法：wrench one's way，表示用力强行通过
  - 例：They had wrenched their way through the boarded-up doors.
- **take a sharp turn**（短语动词）
  - 含义：急转弯
  - 结构 / 用法：take a turn 转弯，sharp 修饰急促
  - 例：Every now and then Uncle Vernon would take a sharp turn and drive in the opposite direction.
