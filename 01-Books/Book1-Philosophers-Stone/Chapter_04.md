# Chapter 4 — The Keeper Of The Keys

BOOM. They knocked again. Dudley jerked awake.

“Where’s the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="fc330154-c234-4c0e-8657-430e9dce9c6d">cannon</mark>?” he said stupidly.

There was a crash behind them and Uncle Vernon came <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="0d2a3970-2101-430f-9137-777ff2fbfbe2">skidding into</mark> the room. He was holding a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="898ac5e2-442e-48b7-9ebe-15f11f149881">rifle</mark> in his hands — now they knew what had been in the long, thin package he had brought with them.

“Who’s there?” he shouted. “I warn you — I’m <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="adde27d9-78f2-41f4-9131-76d7e28fb190">armed</mark>!”

<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="00a84bc2-569c-4f0e-8953-c24378dc93f4">There was a pause</mark>. Then —

SMASH!

The door <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="35753a66-3b4c-4060-ac55-ca94dec05f14">was hit with such force</mark> that it <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a6c58921-9858-4a78-b126-7e7aac56c2c0">swung</mark> clean off its <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c680eaf8-1025-4fa3-a394-fcb489c62c11">hinges</mark> and with a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1001b2df-285e-40cb-828b-9ad5e34091ed">deafening</mark> crash <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a7cf4f96-1576-4edf-a05a-bd8d7dc865a1">landed flat</mark> on the floor.

A <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ed7f7bb5-8fa5-4b3a-b3b2-10fd84ab5675">giant</mark> of a man was standing in the doorway. His face was almost completely hidden by a long, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="9d2306ab-4e69-42a6-a01c-5fd0241210cd">shaggy</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="b8ea0ac7-6039-48c8-b3b2-3590e8bff53f">mane</mark> of hair and a wild, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a48bb8eb-94f0-4651-8ac3-a453ce13f24b">tangled</mark> beard, but you could make out his eyes, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7c4fe067-6285-4a5f-aba4-e41da2dfdc9e">glinting</mark> like black <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="4530b94f-be12-46b7-b80f-b5f28f63713c">beetles</mark> under all the hair.

The giant <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="8020205b-2e6f-4b37-80fa-d36333163d62">squeezed</mark> his way into the hut, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="9d3906c4-749e-497c-bf9d-938d6baf864f">stooping</mark> so that his head just brushed the ceiling. He bent down, picked up the door, and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="b1fe766d-18ad-4126-bdb0-72a2441d2bf0">fitted it</mark> easily back into its frame. The noise of the storm outside <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="71a438a7-d3a0-490d-b30b-f97262e6e583">dropped a little</mark>. He turned to look at them all.

“Couldn’t make us a cup o’ tea, could yeh? It’s not been an easy journey. …”

He <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="03d2a458-d64c-4a4d-a08e-0105c5e8b43e">strode</mark> over to the sofa where Dudley sat frozen with fear.

“Budge up, yeh great <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="bcef8f6e-4e4b-4bcd-926c-f74ca81fc4e8">lump</mark>,” said the stranger.

Dudley <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7d28bde3-e9a9-49f8-8563-024f095d7cdf">squeaked</mark> and ran to hide behind his mother, who was <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="94046a06-5533-4f50-a08f-d6b14f180ab4">crouching</mark>, terrified, behind Uncle Vernon.

“An’ here’s Harry!” said the giant.

Harry looked up into the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="42c0780f-c7c1-4af4-9d65-fdee6df142ed">fierce</mark>, wild, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="912c0e07-ebba-4785-9683-6398535d2b12">shadowy</mark> face and saw that the beetle eyes were <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2fe550a1-4b02-4d09-aed6-ea9c4e0ad645">crinkled</mark> in a smile.

“Las’ time I saw you, you was only a baby,” said the giant. “Yeh look a lot like yer dad, but yeh’ve got yer mom’s eyes.”

Uncle Vernon made a funny <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="aab62024-7298-4a65-b87c-a85464c281d5">rasping</mark> noise.

“I demand that you leave at once, sir!” he said. “You are <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="be8124ff-fcd7-4dd9-b0f9-cc5c4155f2b6">breaking and entering</mark>!”

“Ah, shut up, Dursley, yeh great <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="eb0a749f-8d8f-449b-b8a8-ce7a8508eb69">prune</mark>,” said the giant; he reached over the back of the sofa, jerked the gun out of Uncle Vernon’s hands, bent it into a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c7e71a43-a5ba-42fc-b40f-c0160de399f0">knot</mark> as easily as if it had been made of <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6e285e08-5f26-4d9a-b694-3c58922f06d7">rubber</mark>, and threw it into a corner of the room.

Uncle Vernon made another <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="d833e316-8670-49d2-978e-e1d293790b36">funny noise</mark>, like a mouse being <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7cdb4628-58f1-4926-a90c-0e98b6d61f1c">trodden</mark> on.

“Anyway — Harry,” said the giant, turning his back on the Dursleys, “a very happy birthday to yeh. Got summat fer yeh here — I mighta sat on it at some point, but it’ll taste all right.”

From an inside pocket of his black overcoat he pulled a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="09fbbdc8-da60-4a51-915a-c22f8ec592d0">slightly</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="fd5759fd-1e6f-4e10-8c26-17b860b7d97d">squashed</mark> box. Harry opened it with trembling fingers. Inside was a large, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="751531f3-463a-489c-918e-08caaae7754c">sticky</mark> chocolate cake with Happy Birthday Harry written on it in green <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="645eabfe-121b-492b-970b-ce6b91f8d11f">icing</mark>.

Harry looked up at the giant. He meant to say thank you, but the words got lost on the way to his mouth, and what he said instead was, “Who are you?”

The giant <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="82d2673a-ad95-4b0d-8ed7-35c25c051d7f">chuckled</mark>.

“True, I haven’t introduced meself. Rubeus Hagrid, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="1b2b859b-768e-4290-b7c9-5c11c647c46f">Keeper of Keys and Grounds</mark> at Hogwarts.”

He held out an <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="29c3fad2-8283-43b7-927e-a388da216ce5">enormous</mark> hand and shook Harry’s whole arm.

“What about that tea then, eh?” he said, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="da2d1cbb-07c9-4332-bd80-230be18b5bbf">rubbing</mark> his hands together. “I’d not say no ter summat stronger if yeh’ve got it, mind.”

His eyes fell on the empty <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1d8964b5-4f88-40c8-af50-fab65e10d5aa">grate</mark> with the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d4a3fc47-a991-4a18-8915-0cf20781e701">shriveled</mark> crisp packets in it and he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="89ceaa52-1c9b-4f6c-861c-331b7120f799">snorted</mark>. He bent down over the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2d4be225-4d90-47ae-9691-51e338701187">fireplace</mark>; they couldn’t see what he was doing but when he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="40e8fa35-59b5-460a-8ac1-4c4cab2bd7d4">drew back</mark> a second later, there was a roaring fire there. It filled the whole <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="afd52fad-3197-4a5e-a398-e0a5b94e388b">damp</mark> hut with <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="55494186-1b3d-4dd7-92d8-8672ffd5f468">flickering</mark> light and Harry felt the warmth wash over him as though he’d <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="25d3d7f8-7174-463b-943c-b21ae35a21cc">sunk into</mark> a hot bath.

The giant sat back down on the sofa, which <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="79cda993-6a22-4d35-8583-78dee1a57832">sagged</mark> under his weight, and began taking all sorts of things out of the pockets of his coat: a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1ec3af13-301b-469f-8c84-b016ecb3d57b">copper</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="dc79d6ca-7f62-4255-b330-3cc7407219ab">kettle</mark>, a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2140e635-d918-4a5c-b430-84de83b76924">squashy</mark> package of sausages, a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="752985e1-234d-4d9b-b48a-b4a20f7475e6">poker</mark>, a teapot, several <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="fa151ed4-c928-4e87-88e5-5e8bff990a35">chipped</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="56bea214-05de-4962-b487-83a71eaf7b1f">mugs</mark>, and a bottle of some <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7ec7e0ed-bf7a-4909-8abe-49d51d59f1b0">amber</mark> liquid that he took a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="3ce50fea-2cba-408a-b289-749434278a56">swig</mark> from before starting to make tea. Soon the hut was full of the sound and smell of <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="b202f6fa-9e4c-493e-832b-ab8e67aa296d">sizzling</mark> sausage. Nobody said a thing while the giant was working, but as he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ff256cdb-935e-4d13-9490-01f3f145e5f9">slid</mark> the first six fat, juicy, slightly burnt sausages from the poker, Dudley <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="df1f1f91-6b42-4be2-bdcc-fe282207c86f">fidgeted</mark> a little. Uncle Vernon said sharply, “Don’t touch anything he gives you, Dudley.”

The giant chuckled <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="141a002e-0b0d-45c9-a055-51a09f4628ad">darkly</mark>.

“Yer great puddin’ of a son don’ need fattenin’ anymore, Dursley, don’ worry.”

He passed the sausages to Harry, who was so hungry he had never tasted anything so wonderful, but he still couldn’t take his eyes off the giant. Finally, as nobody seemed about to explain anything, he said, “I’m sorry, but I still don’t really know who you are.”

The giant took a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6bfdab43-4238-41fb-b2a0-886b44b23e4e">gulp</mark> of tea and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d055da61-25c9-47e5-aabe-484076c0174c">wiped</mark> his mouth with the back of his hand.

“Call me Hagrid,” he said, “everyone does. An’ like I told yeh, I’m Keeper of Keys at Hogwarts — yeh’ll know all about Hogwarts, o’ course.”

“Er — no,” said Harry.

Hagrid looked shocked.

“Sorry,” Harry said quickly.

“Sorry?” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="b330e49a-b1bd-4a0d-a1ff-c46556911a45">barked</mark> Hagrid, turning to stare at the Dursleys, who <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="305ee315-21cc-4396-b849-f6f96ea0ba7b">shrank</mark> back into the shadows. “It’s them as should be sorry! I knew yeh weren’t gettin’ yer letters but I never thought yeh wouldn’t even know abou’ Hogwarts, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="fb5402d5-ba07-4a56-9e46-17264be49249">fer cryin’ out loud!</mark> Did yeh never wonder where yer parents learned it all?”

“All what?” asked Harry.

“ALL WHAT?” Hagrid <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="241b05fd-6dbe-49d2-9404-e81ded399e94">thundered</mark>. “Now wait jus’ one second!”

He had <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="71501e63-9538-4835-968a-41235a7b4644">leapt to his feet</mark>. In his anger he seemed to fill the whole hut. The Dursleys were <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="df9fe650-741c-4878-abff-d97f60c9e438">cowering</mark> against the wall.

“Do you mean ter tell me,” he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a8331ad9-51c6-4374-88fb-274b88751400">growled</mark> at the Dursleys, “that this boy — this boy! — knows nothin’ abou’ — about ANYTHING?”

<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-pink" data-id="e1650a0c-541b-4a8b-8e39-9c9e16844d97">Harry thought this was going a bit far. He had been to school, after all, and his marks weren’t bad.</mark>

“I know some things,” he said. “I can, you know, do math and stuff.”

But Hagrid simply waved his hand and said, “About our world, I mean. Your world. My world. Yer parents’ world.”

“What world?”

Hagrid looked as if he was about to <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="8372fccf-a7a2-45cb-a9da-e44277b6078b">explode</mark>.

“DURSLEY!” he boomed.

Uncle Vernon, who had gone very pale, whispered something that sounded like “Mimblewimble.” Hagrid stared wildly at Harry.

“But yeh must know about yer mom and dad,” he said. “I mean, they’re famous. You’re famous.”

“What? My — my mom and dad weren’t famous, were they?”

“Yeh don’ know … yeh don’ know …” Hagrid ran his fingers through his hair, <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d738e6ef-55b4-483e-babe-73633d764d34">fixing</mark> Harry with a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="0fa4fd4d-5fda-4b6a-88a6-c41d0456f366">bewildered</mark> stare.

“Yeh don’ know what yeh are?” he said finally.

Uncle Vernon suddenly found his voice.

“Stop!” he commanded. “Stop right there, sir! I <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1650d851-36f6-4ae3-9a43-412f1d89b99b">forbid</mark> you to tell the boy anything!”

A braver man than Vernon Dursley would have <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="e92754f1-6d46-46b0-b275-1325956e3bd2">quailed</mark> under the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="2eb44aed-08c5-4b72-8d3a-70df5aec43cc">furious</mark> look Hagrid now gave him; when Hagrid spoke, his every <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="89b20bf5-923c-48c7-bc2b-628cebad6cf1">syllable</mark> trembled with <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a4e0482f-da52-446b-87ea-0569383aea3e">rage</mark>.

“You never told him? Never told him what was in the letter Dumbledore left fer him? I was there! I saw Dumbledore leave it, Dursley! An’ you’ve kept it from him all these years?”

“Kept what from me?” said Harry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="cb3c4cc1-027a-4524-ae2b-7c8e99a2a2fe">eagerly</mark>.

“STOP! I FORBID YOU!” yelled Uncle Vernon <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="8aa041bc-e9ac-42ad-bdfa-962e398cad87">in panic</mark>.

Aunt Petunia gave a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="fa70b963-003b-4a4b-a82c-d1cf420e5afe">gasp</mark> of horror.

“Ah, go boil yer heads, both of yeh,” said Hagrid. “Harry — yer a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="54b2d116-986d-47dd-8eae-325a67c13ec7">wizard</mark>.”

There was silence inside the hut. Only the sea and the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="473b4a73-838a-4fd7-be0e-f0b9e263c668">whistling</mark> wind could be heard.

“I’m a what?” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="1076c830-2f4c-4bcc-95bb-67abe12abe20">gasped</mark> Harry.

“A wizard, o’ course,” said Hagrid, sitting back down on the sofa, which <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="6525c71b-3c45-4073-a94b-e79f22ea4a60">groaned</mark> and sank even lower, “an’ a thumpin’ good’un, I’d say, once yeh’ve been <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="060d8479-4d95-4c75-8750-d63194911060">trained</mark> up a bit. With a mum an’ dad like yours, what else would yeh be? An’ I reckon it’s abou’ time yeh read yer letter.”

Harry <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c74787be-37c5-4af6-a93e-b30c50ac904f">stretched</mark> out his hand at last to take the yellowish envelope, addressed in emerald green to Mr. H. Potter, The Floor, Hut-on-the-Rock, The Sea. He pulled out the letter and read:



HOGWARTS SCHOOL

of <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="f66a57ea-5b3c-4b67-a218-efd8233091eb">WITCHCRAFT</mark> and <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="47dc1a50-69aa-4ea6-a902-bc64b9980f4f">WIZARDRY</mark>



Headmaster: ALBUS DUMBLEDORE

(Order of Merlin, First Class, Grand Sorc., Chf. Warlock,

<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="caf88e42-f99a-454e-846f-85bcad558a93">Supreme</mark> Mugwump, International Confed. of Wizards)



Dear Mr. Potter,

We are pleased to inform you that you have been accepted at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Please find <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a5393eb2-9eec-4c78-9f96-c364753a0256">enclosed</mark> a list of all necessary books and equipment.

Term begins on September 1. We await your owl by no later than July 31.

Yours sincerely,



Minerva McGonagall,

<mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="75815998-78de-4f81-a0d4-aeb81851d87c">Deputy</mark> <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7dce9dba-c632-46c1-b6cd-c7e63650a088">Headmistress</mark>



Questions exploded inside Harry’s head like fireworks and he couldn’t decide which to ask first. After a few minutes he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="20f7d407-2a35-4597-aedf-461117458cf0">stammered</mark>, “What does it mean, they await my owl?”

“Gallopin’ Gorgons, that reminds me,” said Hagrid, clapping a hand to his forehead with enough force to knock over a <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="0ce8ed72-9c2c-4428-a118-656b039b58bf">cart horse</mark>, and from yet another pocket inside his overcoat he pulled an owl — a real, live, rather <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="7f775825-8da2-4653-97aa-5bf9ea2c57c3">ruffled</mark>-looking owl — a long <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="c7a9cd1f-021b-4b7e-899a-1a50718b21df">quill</mark>, and a roll of <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a0dc6ac7-764c-4ca8-adfc-8c636103564b">parchment</mark>. With his tongue between his teeth he <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="96657655-135e-4299-a8f0-f6af9d5df135">scribbled</mark> a note which Harry could read upside down:



Dear Professor Dumbledore,

Given Harry his letter.

Taking him to buy his things tomorrow.

Weather’s horrible. Hope you’re well.

Hagrid



Hagrid rolled up the note, gave it to the owl, which <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d04d1687-506f-4fea-80a3-da427f536ba6">clamped</mark> it in its <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="3a24eb5e-4c7c-43d3-ba2d-539608aced5f">beak</mark>, went to the door, and threw the owl out into the storm. Then he came back and sat down as though this was as normal as talking on the telephone.

Harry realized his mouth was open and closed it quickly.

“Where was I?” said Hagrid, but at that moment, Uncle Vernon, still <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d6305f4c-d1ef-4e0d-a20b-1b5f4a15f649">ashen-faced</mark> but looking very angry, moved into the <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="ff1e042a-f137-4ad4-8391-d8e8347b173e">firelight</mark>.

“He’s not going,” he said.

Hagrid <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="de8bdccb-43da-4d11-b62c-2bbf8fb65445">grunted</mark>.

“I’d like ter see a great Muggle like you stop him,” he said.

“A what?” said Harry, interested.

“A Muggle,” said Hagrid, “it’s what we call nonmagic <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="a35889cb-1514-407d-ae76-075402e46b35">folk</mark> like them. An’ it’s your bad luck you grew up in a family o’ the biggest Muggles I ever laid eyes on.”

“We swore when we took him in we’d put a stop to that rubbish,” said Uncle Vernon, “swore we’d <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-green" data-id="ad9350d4-0228-48a4-ac58-b7f0e09e773f">stamp it out</mark> of him! Wizard indeed!”

“You knew?” said Harry. “You knew I’m a — a wizard?”

“Knew!” <mark class="eme-highlight eme-h-yellow" data-id="d14a435e-1179-463f-a543-c2114fec0a04">shrieked</mark> Aunt Petunia suddenly. “Knew! Of course we knew!
